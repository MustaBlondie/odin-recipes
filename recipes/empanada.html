Empanadas

This is a dish from Venezuela

Venezuelan empanadas are a delicious turnovers made with a crispy cornmeal or wheat flour dough, filled with various savory fillings such as beef, cheese, chicken, or black beans. They are often deep-fried until golden brown and served as a popular street food or appetizer in Venezuela.

Ingredients

  • cornmeal or wheat flour
  • Water
  • salt
  • vegetable oil (for the dough)
  • filling (optional)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix cornmeal or wheat flour with water and salt to form a smooth dough.
  2. Let the dough rest for about 30 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, prepare the filling by cooking ground beef, onions, peppers, garlic, and spices in a skillet until browned.
  4. Roll out the dough into small circles or discs.
  5. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each dough circle.
  6. Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape and seal the edges by pressing with a fork.
  7. In a deep pan, heat vegetable oil over medium heat.
  8. Fry the empanadas until golden brown on both sides, turning them gently.
  9. Remove the empanadas from the pan and place them on a paper towel to drain excess oil.
  10. Serve the empanadas hot and enjoy their deliciousness!
